Diagnostic Analysis
Why did performance look this way?
Root-cause analysis — dissecting revenue drivers, store disparities, seasonal spikes, and underperforming segments to understand the forces shaping business outcomes.
📈
December Surge Explained
₦113,000
December 2025 was the highest revenue month (+37% vs average), likely driven by holiday gift spending, corporate orders, and year-end events. It also had the highest transaction count (28 orders).
⚠️
April 2026 Revenue Slump
₦41,300
April 2026 is the worst month on record. This may reflect incomplete data (partial month), post-holiday demand slowdown, or external factors affecting customer footfall across all stores.
🥪
Sandwich Category Leads
39.3%
Sandwiches contribute ₦354,500 (39.3% of revenue). High unit price (₦3,000–₦4,000) and consistent demand from Lekki & Victoria Island drive this. Chicken Panini alone makes up ₦140,000.
🗺️
Yaba vs Ikeja Gap
₦56,600
Yaba leads all stores with ₦252,000 while Ikeja trails at ₦195,400 — a ₦56,600 gap. Ikeja has lower Hot Beverage sales (₦43,500 vs Yaba's ₦68,400) suggesting a different customer preference profile.
🍪
Pastry Underperformance
₦114,100
Pastry is the lowest category at ₦114,100 — just 12.6% of revenue. Low unit prices (₦1,000–₦1,500) reduce revenue potential despite decent transaction volumes. Margin may be disproportionately low.
⏰
Afternoon Peak Discovered
₦288,000
Afternoon (2–6 PM) generates the most revenue at ₦288,000 — 31.9% of total. Morning (₦250,500) is second. This defies typical café assumptions and points to a strong post-lunch premium spend culture.
